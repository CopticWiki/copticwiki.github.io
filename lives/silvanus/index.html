The Teachings of Silvanus

Annul every age of childhood; acquire for yourself the courageousness of the mind and the soul; stand firm in war against all follies of the romantic persuasion and evil wickedness, and vainglory, and quarrelsomeness and tiresome envy and wrath and anger and yearning for greed. Keep watch over your camp, and weapons, and spears. Brace yourself with all the soldiers—who are the words, and the commanders—who are the advice, and your capable mind.
